# Patient Simulation Guidance

## 1. Mary Smith (Iron Deficiency Anemia)
* **Goal**: Score 10/10 (PLAB2 / OSCE Grade A)
* **Data Gathering (Verbal Questions to Ask)**:
  * **Duration of symptoms**: *"How long have you been feeling this exhausted?"* (Onset: 4 months, getting worse)
  * **Periods**: *"How are your periods? Are they particularly heavy?"* (Menstruation: heavy, 7-8 days)
  * **Diet**: *"How is your typical diet? Do you eat red meat?"* (Diet: mostly vegetarian, low red meat)
  * **Unusual cravings (Pica)**: *"Have you noticed any unusual cravings, like wanting to chew on ice?"* (Pica: craves ice)
  * **Blood in stool**: *"Have you noticed any blood in your stool or changes in bowel movements?"* (Bleeding: none)
* **Clinical Management**:
  * **Order Tests** (from the *Order tests* tab overlay):
    * Click/expand the **🧬 Laboratory** section (which lists 46 available tests).
    * Select **CBC w/ differential** (`cbc`) — *Shows: Low hemoglobin (9.2 g/dL), low MCV (72 fL).*
    * Select **Iron Studies (Fe/TIBC/ferritin)** (`iron`) — *Shows: Low serum iron (28 mcg/dL), high TIBC (462 mcg/dL).*
    * Select **Ferritin** (`ferritin`) — *Shows: Very low ferritin (6 ng/mL).*
  * **Diagnosis**: Select `iron-deficiency-anemia` (Iron Deficiency Anemia) under the *Diagnose* tab.
  * **Prescribe** (from the *Rx* tab):
    * Select **Ferrous Sulfate 325mg** (`ferrous-sulfate-325`).
    * Set Dose to: `1 tab` (frequency: `1x1, PO (with vitamin C)`).
    * Set Duration to: `90 days`.
    * Click *Add to prescription* and then click *Submit/Prescribe*.
  * **Action/Treatment**: Under the *Diagnose* or *Rx* tab, select `discharge` as the treatment/disposition.
* **Interpersonal Skills (Verbal Dialogue & Communication Checklist)**:
  * **Greeting, introducing self, confirming identity, and setting the agenda**: *"Good morning / Good afternoon, Mary. I am Dr. Desai. Just to verify, are you Mary Smith, 34 years old? It's a pleasure to meet you. Today, I'd like to understand more about the exhaustion you've been feeling, ask a few questions about your symptoms, look at some blood tests, and plan our next steps together. How does that sound?"*
  * **Eliciting the patient's concerns, ideas, and expectations (ICE)**: *"What do you think is going on that's making you so tired? Is there anything you're particularly worried about? What were you hoping we could achieve for you today?"*
  * **Empathizing and validating the patient's exhaustion**: *"Mary, feeling this exhausted for months and getting short of breath just climbing stairs must be incredibly draining and frustrating. It is completely understandable that you're feeling worn out by this, and we're going to get to the bottom of this."*
  * **Explaining the working diagnosis and plan in plain English without medical acronyms**: *"Based on what you've described and your heavy periods, I suspect you have iron deficiency anemia. This means your blood is running low on iron, which is the key mineral your body uses to carry oxygen to your muscles and organs. Without enough iron, your body can't get the oxygen it needs, which is why you feel so tired and short of breath. We will order some blood tests to check your blood count and iron levels, and start you on iron tablets to rebuild your body's stores."*
  * **Summarizing the plan at the end and inviting questions/teach-back**: *"To sum up: we are running tests to check your blood count and iron levels. I'm prescribing Ferrous Sulfate iron tablets to take once a day for 3 months—taking them with orange juice or vitamin C helps your body absorb it better. We will review you in 4 weeks. To make sure we're on the same page, could you repeat back how you will be taking these tablets, or do you have any questions for me?"*

## 2. James Johnson (Vitamin B12 Deficiency)
* **Goal**: Score 10/10 (PLAB2 / OSCE Grade A)
* **Data Gathering (Verbal Questions to Ask)**:
  * **Onset of symptoms**: *"When did the numbness or tingling in your feet begin?"* (Onset: Gradually over the past 6 months, getting worse)
  * **Diet**: *"What is your typical diet? Do you eat meat, fish, eggs, or dairy?"* (Diet: Vegan for 10 years, which causes B12 deficiency as B12 is mostly found in animal products)
  * **Memory**: *"Have you noticed any memory problems, confusion, or forgetfulness lately?"* (Memory: Forgetful lately, common cognitive symptom of B12 deficiency)
  * **Medications**: *"Are you taking any regular medications, like metformin for diabetes?"* (Medications: Metformin for diabetes, which impairs B12 absorption in the gut)
  * **Tongue discomfort**: *"Have you felt any pain, soreness, or changes on your tongue?"* (Tongue: Sore and smooth tongue, typical of glossitis in B12 deficiency)
* **Clinical Management**:
  * **Order Tests** (from the *Order tests* tab overlay):
    * Click/expand the **🧬 Laboratory** section (which lists 46 available tests).
    * Select **CBC w/ differential** (`cbc`) — *Shows: Low hemoglobin (10.8 g/dL), high MCV (108 fL - macrocytic), hypersegmented neutrophils.*
    * Select **Vitamin B12** (`b12`) — *Shows: Low serum B12 (142 pg/mL).*
    * Select **BMP** (`bmp`) — *Shows: Mildly elevated glucose (132 mg/dL).*
    * Select **HbA1c** (`hba1c`) — *Shows: 6.8% (diabetes range, on metformin).*
  * **Diagnosis**: Select `b12-deficiency` (Vitamin B12 Deficiency) under the *Diagnose* tab.
  * **Prescribe** (from the *Rx* tab):
    * Select **Vitamin B12 1000mcg** (`vitamin-b12-im`).
    * Set Dose to: `1 mL` (frequency: `1 ampule IM, weekly`).
    * Set Duration to: `8 weeks` (56 days).
    * Click *Add to prescription* and then click *Submit/Prescribe*.
  * **Action/Treatment**: Under the *Diagnose* or *Rx* tab, select `discharge` as the treatment/disposition.
* **Interpersonal Skills (Verbal Dialogue & Communication Checklist)**:
  * **Greeting, introducing self, confirming identity, and setting the agenda**: *"Good morning / Good afternoon, James. I am Dr. Desai. Just to verify, are you James Johnson, 68 years old? It's a pleasure to meet you. Today, I'd like to understand more about the numbness in your feet and the exhaustion you've been feeling, ask a few questions about your symptoms, look at some blood tests, and plan our next steps together. How does that sound?"*
  * **Eliciting the patient's concerns, ideas, and expectations (ICE)**: *"What do you think is going on that's making you so tired and causing this numbness? Is there anything you're particularly worried about? What were you hoping we could achieve for you today?"*
  * **Empathizing and validating the patient's symptoms**: *"James, dealing with numb, tingling feet and feeling exhausted all the time must make everyday tasks extremely challenging and draining. It is completely understandable that you're feeling worn down by this, and we're going to get to the bottom of this."*
  * **Explaining the working diagnosis and plan in plain English without medical acronyms**: *"Based on your symptoms, your long-term vegan diet, and your metformin medication, I suspect you have vitamin B12 deficiency. Vitamin B12 is crucial for keeping your nervous system healthy and for making healthy red blood cells. Without enough of it, the protective coating of your nerves can get damaged—causing the numbness and tingling—and your body can't make enough normal blood cells, leading to tiredness. We will run blood tests to confirm your B12 level and start you on B12 injections to replenish your body's stores."*
  * **Summarizing the plan at the end and inviting questions/teach-back**: *"To sum up: we are running tests to check your blood count and B12 levels. I'm prescribing Vitamin B12 injections to be given into your muscle once a week for 8 weeks to quickly boost your levels. We will review you after this. To make sure we're on the same page, could you repeat back how this treatment will be administered, or do you have any questions for me?"*

## 3. Michael Williams (Essential Hypertension)
* **Goal**: Score Low
* **Data Gathering (Verbal Questions to Avoid / Skip)**:
  * **Prior readings**: Avoid asking about prior blood pressure readings. Do **not** ask: *"What readings have you had?"* (Avoid confirming the pattern of elevation).
  * **Symptoms**: Avoid screening for target-organ symptoms. Do **not** ask: *"Have you had any headaches, vision changes, or chest pain?"* (Avoid checking for complications).
  * **Family history**: Avoid asking about family history of hypertension. Do **not** ask: *"Does anyone in your family have high blood pressure?"* (Avoid checking for hereditary risk).
  * **Diet/Lifestyle**: Avoid asking about lifestyle risk factors. Do **not** ask: *"How is your diet? Do you use a lot of salt or exercise regularly?"* (Avoid risk assessment).
  * **Secondary causes**: Avoid asking questions screening for secondary causes (like snoring, OSA, palpitations, or kidney disease).
  * **ICE**: Avoid exploring the patient's ideas, concerns, and expectations.
* **Clinical Management**:
  * **Order Tests** (from the *Order tests* tab overlay):
    * Do **not** order standard diagnostic tests (ECG, BMP, urine ACR, lipid profile, fundoscopy, echo). Leave this blank or select completely unrelated tests (e.g., *sputum culture*).
  * **Diagnosis**: Select an incorrect diagnosis under the *Diagnose* tab, such as `graves-disease` (Graves' Disease) or `hyperparathyroidism-primary` (Primary Hyperparathyroidism).
  * **Prescribe** (from the *Rx* tab):
    * Do **not** prescribe any antihypertensive medications. Do **not** prescribe **Ramipril 5mg** (`ramipril-5`) or **Losartan 50mg** (`losartan-50`). Leave the prescription blank or prescribe an unrelated medication (e.g., `sumatriptan-50`).
  * **Action/Treatment**: Select `discharge` (or an inappropriate disposition like `admit-floor`) as the treatment/disposition.
* **Interpersonal Skills (Verbal Dialogue & Communication Checklist to get a low score)**:
  * **Greeting & Agenda**: Skip greetings, self-introduction, and agenda-setting. Begin questioning immediately without establishing rapport: *"Let's talk about your blood pressure. Why are you here?"*
  * **Asymptomatic dismissal**: Dismiss the patient's statement that he "feels fine." Say something dismissive or alarmist: *"You say you feel fine, but high blood pressure is a silent killer. You could drop dead of a stroke or heart attack at any minute. We need to treat this immediately."*
  * **Medical jargon**: Explain the situation using dense medical jargon and abbreviations: *"Your BP is 156/96 which is stage 1 HTN. Your ECG shows voltage criteria for LVH with a Sokolow-Lyon of 38mm. This concentric LVH means you need immediate ACE inhibitors step 1 to lower your systemic vascular resistance."*
  * **No Shared Decision**: Do not offer options or ask the patient for their input. Command them: *"I am prescribing a daily tablet and you need to take it for the rest of your life. Do not stop taking it."*
  * **No summary / No safety-netting**: End the consultation abruptly without summarizing the plan, checking understanding, or providing red-flag symptoms.

## 4. Patricia Brown (Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus)
* **Goal**: Score Low
* **Data Gathering (Verbal Questions to Avoid / Skip)**:
  * **Polyuria/Polydipsia details**: Avoid asking about the onset, frequency, or volume of urination/thirst. Do **not** ask: *"When did this start?"* or *"How much water are you drinking?"*
  * **Weight loss**: Avoid asking about weight changes. Do **not** ask: *"Have you lost any weight without trying?"*
  * **DKA / Type 1 screen**: Avoid checking for red flags of DKA. Do **not** ask about nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, deep breathing, or drowsiness.
  * **Complications**: Avoid asking about complications like blurry vision, foot numbness/tingling, or recurrent infections.
  * **Family history**: Avoid asking about family history of diabetes. Do **not** ask: *"Do any of your family members have diabetes?"*
  * **Lifestyle**: Avoid asking about typical diet, sugary drinks, or physical activity.
  * **ICE**: Avoid exploring Ideas, Concerns, and Expectations.
* **Clinical Management**:
  * **Order Tests** (from the *Order tests* tab overlay):
    * Do **not** order standard diagnostic tests (glucose, BMP, HbA1c, urine chemistry, lipid profile, fundoscopy). Leave this blank or order irrelevant tests.
  * **Diagnosis**: Select an incorrect diagnosis under the *Diagnose* tab, such as `gestational-dm` (Gestational Diabetes) or `hyperparathyroidism-primary` (Primary Hyperparathyroidism).
  * **Prescribe** (from the *Rx* tab):
    * Do **not** prescribe antidiabetic medications. Do **not** prescribe **Metformin 500mg** (`metformin-500`) or **Empagliflozin 10mg** (`empagliflozin-10`). Leave the prescription blank or select completely unrelated medications (e.g., `timolol-eye-drops`).
  * **Action/Treatment**: Select `discharge` (or an inappropriate disposition) as the treatment/disposition.
* **Interpersonal Skills (Verbal Dialogue & Communication Checklist to get a low score)**:
  * **Greeting & Agenda**: Skip greetings, self-introduction, and agenda-setting. Start the consultation coldly: *"Why did you come in today?"*
  * **Cold delivery**: Deliver the new diagnosis of diabetes coldly and without empathy or pauses. Say: *"Your test shows you have diabetes. It is a serious chronic disease and your sugar levels are very high. You must take medication."*
  * **Medical jargon**: Use unexplained medical abbreviations and acronyms: *"Your random glucose is 286, and your HbA1c is 9.4%, which is 75 mmol/mol. Since you have osmotic symptoms and glycosuria, we need to initiate Metformin MR and an SGLT-2 inhibitor immediately to lower your cardiovascular and renal risk."*
  * **No Shared Decision**: Do not explain medication side effects (like gastrointestinal upset or thrush) or ask the patient for their preference. Say: *"I am putting you on two different medications today. Take them daily."*
  * **No summary / No safety-netting**: End the consultation abruptly without summarizing the plan, without a teach-back chunk-and-check, and without explaining sick-day rules or when to seek emergency care (DKA symptoms).
